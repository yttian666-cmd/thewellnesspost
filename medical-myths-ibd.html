Medical Myths: All about IBD

Inflammatory bowel disease is not the same as IBS, and myths about diet, stress, and "quick cures" can make care harder.

Curated from Medical News Today · Adapted for The Wellness Post

Medical News Today's IBD myth feature explains common misunderstandings around Crohn's disease and ulcerative colitis, including the belief that IBD is simply IBS, caused by stress, or curable through diet alone.

IBD is inflammatoryCrohn's disease and ulcerative colitis involve immune-driven inflammation, not only bowel sensitivity.
Diet is not a cureFood choices may reduce symptoms for some people, but medical treatment is often needed.
Symptoms go beyond gutFatigue, joint pain, skin issues, eye inflammation, and anemia can appear with IBD.

What to watch for

Blood in stool, ongoing diarrhea, unexplained weight loss, fever, persistent pain, and severe fatigue are not symptoms to normalize. They should prompt medical evaluation.

For students, the most useful distinction is this: IBS can be disruptive, but IBD can damage tissue and needs monitoring. The names sound similar, but the conditions are different.
