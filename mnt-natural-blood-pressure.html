15 natural ways to lower blood pressure

Regular movement, sodium awareness, potassium-rich foods and stress management can support healthier blood pressure, especially when combined with medical care when needed.

Curated from Medical News Today · Updated June 24, 2025

High blood pressure raises the risk of heart disease and stroke. Medical News Today lists evidence-informed habits that may help people reduce blood pressure naturally, while noting that some readers still need medication and clinical monitoring.

30 minDaily walking can help lower blood pressure.
PotassiumFresh produce can help balance sodium effects for many people.
StressBreathing and meditation can activate the relaxation response.

What helps most

Exercise is one of the strongest starting points. Regular walking, running or other activity helps the heart pump more efficiently and can lower pressure in the arteries. Reducing sodium, moderating alcohol, stopping smoking and limiting added sugar or refined carbohydrates can also support heart health.

Food choices matter because potassium, magnesium, calcium, berries, cocoa flavonoids and whole foods may all play supporting roles. People taking blood pressure medication should ask a clinician before sharply increasing potassium intake because some medicines can raise potassium levels.

When to seek care

Home habits are not a substitute for urgent treatment. Very high readings, chest pain, shortness of breath, weakness or sudden neurological symptoms require immediate medical help. For routine high readings, readers should keep a log and speak with a doctor or pharmacist.
