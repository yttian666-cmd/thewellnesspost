Everyday Habits for Sensitive Skin

A simple guide to building a calmer skin routine: fewer irritants, better sun protection, and knowing when persistent reactions need medical advice.

Curated from Healthline Program · Topic overview for student readers

Keep it simpleA gentle cleanser, moisturiser, and sunscreen are often more useful than a crowded routine.
Patch testTrying one new product at a time makes it easier to identify what triggers redness, stinging, or itching.
Protect the barrierOver-exfoliating, fragrance-heavy products, and harsh scrubs can make sensitive skin feel worse.

Why this topic matters

Skincare advice online can push constant product changes. Sensitive skin usually needs the opposite: consistency, barrier support, sun protection, and attention to triggers such as fragrance, weather, sweat, or acne treatments.

When to seek help

Persistent rashes, swelling, pain, open skin, or reactions that keep returning should be checked by a clinician. A dermatologist can help distinguish sensitivity from eczema, allergy, rosacea, infection, or another condition.
